# Chrome Profile Management

How to switch between Google accounts in Chrome:

**Method 1: Using the Profile Icon**
1. Look at the top-right corner of your Chrome window for your profile picture (or a colored circle with your initial).
2. Click it.
3. A dropdown menu appears showing all your Chrome profiles/accounts.
4. Click the account you want to switch to. Chrome will open a new window with that profile.

**Method 2: Keyboard Shortcut**
- Press **Ctrl + Shift + M** (Windows/Linux) or **Cmd + Shift + M** (Mac) to open the profile switcher instantly.

**Method 3: From the Chrome Menu**
1. Click the three-dot menu (⋮) in the top-right corner.
2. Select the profile name at the top of the menu, or go to **Settings → You and Google → Other Chrome profiles** to switch.

**Tips for Managing Multiple Accounts:**
- **Add a new account:** Click your profile icon → **Add** → Sign in with a different Google account or continue without signing in.
- **Customize profiles:** Give each profile a distinct name and color so you can tell them apart at a glance.
- **Guest mode:** Use the "Browse as Guest" option if you just need temporary access without signing in.
- **Desktop shortcuts:** You can create separate desktop shortcuts for each profile so they open directly without switching.

**Note:** Each Chrome profile keeps its own bookmarks, history, passwords, and extensions completely separate. If you just want to switch Google accounts *within the same profile* (for Gmail, Drive, etc.), click your avatar in the top-right of the Google website and select "Add account" or "Switch account" instead.
